How to use the notes
Physics material is unlike a fictional story. The content is usually highly interlinked, making it easy to lose track of the whole picture as a student continues reading. It is therefore strongly recommended that after every section or a few sections, pause and review the material. This not only improves retention of knowledge but the retained knowledge forms a foundation for additional knowledge to be built upon. The notes are thus designed with a right hand column containing the summarised key points to facilitate this pause and review learning strategy.

I shall explain another important use of the summarised points in the right hand column. Many students find it hard to handle explanation questions. Sometimes not knowing where to start. For example, take the question 'Explain why work has to be done to bring a car to a stop?'. The question doesn't even make sense in everyday language but in physics, 'work done' comes with a meaning and your understanding of that meaning will allow you to make sense of the question and thus allow you to 'explain' it. Key idea - work done can be understood as a means of energy transfer and doing negative work on an object means taking energy away from the object. When a car slows down to a stop, it loses KE and this corresponds to some force doing negative work on the car.  The key idea mentioned is one of the many summarised key points listed in the right hand column. They form the fundamental building blocks (like Lego blocks) I would use to construct answers to explanation questions.

Why are the notes so wordy?
Conceptual understanding is about meaning as explained in my home page. Highly summarised materials tend to have just pictures, mind-maps, graphs, tables supplemented with short phrases or sentences. This makes the content look a lot more manageable and who doesn't like short-cuts? Unfortunately I think the highly summarised notes are good only if you already have good understanding but no good if you want to start gaining understanding. This is why my notes are wordy - to describe the meaning I see in the equations, laws, key words etc. After you have gained that understanding, use the summarised points in the right hand column for future quick reference.
